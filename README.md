This repository contains all materials related to my Master's thesis examining 
how contemporary films (2015-2025) construct ideologies about autism through 
representational practices. The research employs Critical Discourse Analysis 
to investigate how films naturalize particular understandings of autism while 
foreclosing alternatives, and what power relations shape these representations.

RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

RQ1: How do film representations of autism (2015-2025) construct ideologies 
     about disability and difference, and what power relations shape these 
     representations?

RQ2: What storytelling and representational choices in contemporary films 
     challenge dominant ideas about autism, and what production factors 
     support the creation of these films?
